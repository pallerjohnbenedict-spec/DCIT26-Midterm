History Of Cvsu

Founder of Cavite State University

The Cavite State University (CvSU) was not founded by one single individual. Its origins trace back to the Indang Intermediate School, established in 1906 by American Thomasites, and it has since evolved through several transformations. A significant role in the institution's development was played by Don Severino de las Alas, who donated land for the establishment of the intermediate school. In recognition of his generosity, the school later became known as Don Severino National Agricultural School in 1958, and then Don Severino Agricultural College (DSAC) in 1964. The current CvSU name was adopted later, as it evolved into a state university.

About Cavite State University

The Cavite State University (CvSU) has its humble beginnings in 1906 as the Indang Intermediate School with the American Thomasites as the first teachers. Several transformations in the name of the school took place: Indang Farm School in 1918, Indang Rural High School in 1927, and Don Severino National Agriculture School in 1958.

In 1964, the school was converted into a State College by virtue of Republic Act 3917 and became known as Don Severino Agricultural College (DSAC). On January 22, 1998, by virtue of Republic Act No. 8468, DSAC was converted into Cavite State University.

In 2001, Cavite College of Fisheries (CACOF) in Naic, and Cavite College of Arts and Trade (CCAT) in Rosario were integrated into the University. Today, CvSU has 11 campuses across the province. The main campus, located in Indang, Cavite, spans approximately 70 hectares and is named Don Severino delas Alas Campus.

CvSU provides excellent, equitable, and relevant educational opportunities in the arts, sciences, and technology. It produces professional, skilled, and morally upright individuals prepared for global competitiveness.

The University offers nearly 100 programs at the undergraduate and graduate levels, serves more than 25,000 students, and employs over 1,200 faculty and staff. It is accredited by TESDA and authorized under ETEEAP to confer academic degrees. CvSU is also a research hub for agriculture, coffee, ICT, and renewable energy in Region IV-A.

CvSU upholds the core values of Truth, Excellence, and Service, and envisions itself as the “premier University in historic Cavite recognized for excellence in the development of globally competitive and morally upright individuals.”

Mission

To provide excellent, equitable, and relevant educational opportunities in the arts, sciences, and technology through quality instruction and responsive research and development activities.

Vision

The premier university in historic Cavite recognized for excellence in the development of globally competitive and morally upright individuals.

Core Values

  • Truth – Upholding integrity and honesty in all endeavors.
  • Excellence – Committing to the highest standards in education and service.
  • Service – Demonstrating concern for community, country, and environment.
